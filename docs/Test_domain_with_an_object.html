Results of the Anisotropic Inverse Problem EIT with Hua virtual electrode

Anisotropic domain with an Square object phantom - Angle 0°

This page presents the results of the anisotropic inverse problem using the Hua virtual electrode model in Electrical Impedance Tomography (EIT). The figures below show:

  • The conductivity distribution of the phantom;
  • The electric field behavior caused by anisotropy;
  • The reconstructed conductivity obtained from the inverse problem;
  • The influence of different regularization parameters λ.

Rotação do sistema de coordenadas
$$ \sigma = \begin{bmatrix} \sigma_{xx} & \sigma_{xy} \\ \sigma_{xy} & \sigma_{yy} \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ R = \begin{bmatrix} \cos\theta & -\sin\theta \\ \sin\theta & \cos\theta \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ c=\cos\theta $$ $$ s=\sin\theta $$ $$ \sigma' = R \sigma R^T $$ $$ R\sigma \begin{bmatrix} c & -s \\ s & c \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \sigma_{xx} & \sigma_{xy} \\ \sigma_{xy} & \sigma_{yy} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} c\sigma_{xx}-s\sigma_{xy} & c\sigma_{xy}-s\sigma_{yy} \\ s\sigma_{xx}+c\sigma_{xy} & s\sigma_{xy}+c\sigma_{yy} \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ \sigma' = (R\sigma)R^T = \sigma'_{xy} $$ # $$ \sigma'_{xy} (\sigma_{xx}-\sigma_{yy})sc + \sigma_{xy}(c^2-s^2) $$ Nos eixos principais: $$ \sigma'_{xy}=0 $$ Então: $$ (\sigma_{xx}-\sigma_{yy})sc + \sigma_{xy}(c^2-s^2) =0 $$ Usando identidades trigonométricas Usamos: $$ \sin(2\theta)=2sc $$ e $$ \cos(2\theta)=c^2-s^2 $$ Substituindo: $$ \frac{\sigma_{xx}-\sigma_{yy}}{2}\sin(2\theta) + \sigma_{xy}\cos(2\theta) =0 $$ Dividindo por $$\cos(2\theta)$$: # $$ \frac{\sigma_{xx}-\sigma_{yy}}{2}\tan(2\theta) -\sigma_{xy} $$ Logo: # $$ \tan(2\theta) \frac{2\sigma_{xy}}{\sigma_{xx}-\sigma_{yy}} $$ Aplicando arco-tangente: $$ 2\theta= \arctan\left( \frac{2\sigma_{xy}} {\sigma_{xx}-\sigma_{yy}} \right) $$ Portanto: $$ \boxed{ \theta= \frac{1}{2} \arctan\left( \frac{2\sigma_{xy}} {\sigma_{xx}-\sigma_{yy}} \right) } $$
